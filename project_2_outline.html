1 Requesting the DHS Data

For accessing and downloading the Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) data in preparation for Project 2, I had to write a short outline for the project. This is what I included in my proposal:

I am in an Agent Based Modeling course at William & Mary, where I am constructing a model of Eswatini. This data will be used to produce a synthetic population of Eswatini as a preliminary step. Using results from this model, I will describe land use and health care demand, as well as describe movement within Eswatini.

I also requested the GPS data from the DHS website. This was my short justification for requesting this additional data:

In addition to generating a synthetic population, I hope to produce accurate geographic population models for Eswatini. This data set would help improve accuracy over a model based solely on survey data.

Im very excited to start working with this data! I think there will be some interesting things to explore in terms of demand for different infrastructure elements. Additionally, in Project 1 I saw some very strange trends in terms of the placement of health care facilities. Im hoping to investigate this further, ideally with putting together some quantitative measure of physical accessibility to health care.

2 Basic Work with the Data

DHS approved both data sources pretty quick! When initially opening the household data, I was pretty taken aback by the ultra-wide format (more than 4000 columns). Unfortunately, the data didnt come along with a Stata .do file, so it was a little more difficult to locate the variables of interest.

Heres what I was able to find:

  • The number of household members is in hv009.
  • The sex of household members is in hv104_01 to hv104_34.
  • The age of household members is in hv105_01 to hv105_34.
  • The region identifier (first-level administrative subdivisions) is in hv_024.
  • I have yet to find an identifier for tinkhundla (second-level administrative subdivisions), which makes it a little more difficult to spatially locate the households of interest.
  • Sample weights are in hv005.

I also downloaded 2006 population raster data from WorldPop to match up with the survey, which was conducted in 2006.

3 A Quick Plot

I quickly plotted the geospatial data to see the distribution of clusters across Eswatini. There are 275 total clusters, and Ive seperated urban and rural clusters by color.

Even though I wasnt able to find a more granular location identifier, I should be able to figure out which clusters are in Mkhiweni using this data and filter those out of the survey data!
